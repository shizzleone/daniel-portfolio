Project overview

Making wellness feel supportive, personal, and possible on imperfect days.

Grounded reframes habit building around self-awareness and recovery. Instead of punishing missed streaks, it adapts recommendations to energy, mood, available time, and personal goals.

My roleLead Product Designer

Research strategy, product framing, interaction design, prototyping, testing, and visual direction.

AudienceBusy knowledge workers

People balancing work, relationships, health goals, and changing energy levels.

Outcome focusSustainable engagement

Build repeat wellness behavior without guilt, overload, or rigid routines.

The challenge

Most wellness apps turn self-care into another performance metric.

01

Streak anxiety

Missing one day can make progress feel erased and discourage people from returning.

02

Generic recommendations

Programs ignore how energy, time, and emotional needs change from day to day.

03

Too much to manage

Dense dashboards and endless content add cognitive load when users already feel depleted.

Research synthesis

People need permission to adjust, not pressure to perform.

78%

Want flexible routines

Participants preferred options that could adapt to the time and energy they had available.

3 min

Is enough to begin

Small, clearly framed actions lowered the barrier to starting a wellness practice.

Recovery

Matters as much as consistency

People wanted help returning after disruption without shame or lost progress.

Experience principles

A wellness product designed to meet people where they are.

01

Ask before advising

Daily guidance begins with mood, energy, and available time.

02

Celebrate return

Progress language rewards coming back instead of protecting streaks.

03

Make small feel meaningful

Every practice explains the benefit of even a few intentional minutes.

04

Show patterns gently

Insights support reflection without turning wellbeing into a scorecard.

Core experience flow

From self-awareness to the right action in under a minute.

Check in→Understand today→Choose a small action→Complete practice→Reflect & adapt
